A door for backyard observers

Start with the sky you can see from where you are.

This demonstration shows the kind of small, creator-owned community door FYN can build today.

Big Sky Backyard Astronomy is for people who are learning the night sky with binoculars, a small telescope, a camera, or no equipment at all. It is not a social network, club-management system, event platform, or shared publishing space.

The purpose of this door is simple: describe who the space is for, collect a few useful resources, and point people toward the places where astronomy communities and reliable information already exist.

What this demo proves

A FYN site can explain a community, organize creator-selected links, and publish multiple static pages. It does not create accounts, store visitors, rank content, search a central directory, or host community conversations.

You may belong here if…

  • You are learning constellations from a backyard, porch, campsite, or city park.
  • You want practical observing resources without buying a large amount of equipment first.
  • You are looking for a local club, public star party, or experienced observer.
  • You care about dark skies and responsible outdoor lighting.

No tracking. No data collected. Static site generated by Finding Your Neighborhood.
